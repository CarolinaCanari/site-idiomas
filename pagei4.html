Present Simple

We use present simple to talk about things that are generally true in daily life:

  • habits and routines
  • facts and feelings

The order of the phrases are: Pronoun + Verb + Complement.

I/ You/ We/ They

  • I wake up
  • I eat lunch
  • I make a cup of coffee

Be careful with the verbs do, go and have after singular nouns (e.g. London, my teacher) and he / she / it in positive sentences:

  • do — does
  • go — goes
  • have — has

When we talk about singular nouns (He, She, It) we need to change de verbs:

He/She/It

  • She wakes up
  • She goes home
  • He makes a cup of coffee

The most situations we add - “s”:

  • Start= starts
  • Work= works
  • Play= plays

If the verb ends in consonant + “y” you have to change the - “y” to - “i” and add - “es”:

  • Try= tries
  • Study= studies

If the verb ends in -sh, -ch, -x, -ss, you add - “es”:

  • Wash= washes
  • Catch= catches
  • Watch= watches

To make questions and negative phrases we useDo (I, you, we, they) and Does (he, she, it).

Be careful because in negative and in questions, using does to he, she, it, we do not change the verb.

In questions, the order also changes: Do/Does + Pronoun + Verb + Complement.
